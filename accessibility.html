Accessibility statement for SOLAR

This statement applies to the SOLAR demonstration workbench, registry and front door.

SOLAR is a local-first intelligence workbench that runs in the browser on the user’s own machine. This statement is written to reflect the format set out in the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (“PSBAR 2018”), against the WCAG 2.2 AA standard.

Honest status. SOLAR is a demonstration build on synthetic data. It has not yet been independently audited. This statement is a truthful self-assessment, not a claim of certified conformance. An independent WCAG 2.2 AA and assistive-technology audit is required before any operational or procurement submission.

Compliance status

This application is partially compliant with the WCAG 2.2 AA standard, due to the non-compliances and the not-yet-audited status listed below.

What you can do

We aim to let you:

  • operate the core workflow — add intelligence, review extracted entities, and inspect the resulting network — using a keyboard alone;
  • read a text equivalent of the visual link chart and map. Below the timeline, the “Text equivalent of chart & map” panel (toggle with the button, or press Alt + T) presents every entity, relationship and location as navigable tables, driven from the same data as the chart;
  • select any entity from that panel with Enter; the selection is announced via a live region and is synchronised with the chart, map, timeline and inspector;
  • use the interface with a screen reader for the report registry and the structured panels.

Non-accessible content

Known limitations

  • The link chart and map are rendered to canvas and are not themselves exposed to assistive technology. The text-equivalent panel is provided as the conforming alternative; the canvases carry an accessible name pointing to it. (WCAG 1.1.1, 4.1.2.)
  • The timeline is canvas-based. Its events are represented as dated entries within the entity and relationship tables of the text-equivalent panel; a dedicated timeline table is a planned improvement.
  • Map tiles are supplied by an external basemap service and may not meet all contrast expectations; location data is fully available as text in the equivalent panel and does not depend on the tiles.
  • No independent audit has yet been carried out. Further issues may exist that only a formal assistive-technology audit will surface.

Preparation of this statement

This statement was prepared on the basis of a self-assessment of the demonstration build: automated checks, code review, a keyboard-operation review of the core workflow, and a review of the semantic structure exposed to screen readers. It has not been verified by an external body.
